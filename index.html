Fahim Anzum

Alberta Innovates and Eyes High Doctoral Scholar · Computer Science Ph.D. Candidate · University of Calgary · fahim.anzum@ucalgary.ca

I am a fourth-year Computer Science Ph.D. candidate and an Alberta Innovates Doctoral Scholar at the University of Calgary, Alberta, Canada. My advisor is Professor Dr. Marina L Gavrilova and we are at the Biometric Technologies Laboratory (BT Lab).

My research is driven by the vision of harnessing users' dynamic behavioral and psychological traits from online social media to build Trustworthy and Explainable AI (TXAI) frameworks, enabling user-centered decision support and human-machine collaboration. I have pioneered novel approaches that fuse interpretable handcrafted features with large language model (LLM)-driven contextual representations, achieving state-of-the-art precision in analyzing social behaviors through emotion detection in social media. Additionally, I have developed an LLM-based social media mining strategy that uncovers Canadians' trust perceptions in healthcare, offering actionable insights for medical institutions. My interdisciplinary research extends to TXAI frameworks for mental health interventions and personalized policy recommendations, bridging AI, psychology, and human-computer interaction. At the BT Lab, I have spearheaded initiatives to embed user trust factors into AI algorithms, prioritizing fairness, transparency, and equity. See my Research Statement and Publications for more details.

My research contributions have been recognized in high-impact Computer Science and transdisciplinary venues, including IEEE Access, Springer Nature, IEEE Cyberworlds, and IEEE Human-Machine Systems. My recent work on a bi-modal architecture for interpretable emotion detection earned the Best Paper Award at the 2024 IEEE International Conference on Human-Machine Systems (ICHMS), highlighting its contribution to advancing Trustworthy Human-Machine Teaming for Effective Decision-Making.

Fahim Anzum

Alberta Innovates and Eyes High Doctoral Scholar · Computer Science Ph.D. Student · University of Calgary · fahim.anzum@ucalgary.ca

I am a Computer Science Ph.D. student at the University of Calgary, Alberta, Canada, and currently conducting research under Dr. Marina Gavrilova's supervision in the Biometric Technologies Laboratory. Before joining the Ph.D. program, I earned my master's degree in August 2021 from the University of Calgary, where I conducted research under the supervision of Dr. Mario Costa Sousa and Dr. Usman Alim. My master’s research was fully funded by Mitacs-Accelerate Graduate Research Internship Program. Throughout my master's study, I also worked with Suncor Energy Inc., the primary industrial partner of my research project, as a research intern.

My research interests are mostly centered around applying machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) in various areas i.e., online social media, biometric technology, healthcare, autonomous driving, computer vision, human-in-the-loop computing, etc. I am also passionate about investigating and modeling ML methods for human-computer interaction (HCI), such as people's behavior modeling based on their daily interaction with social media and smart devices. I want to leverage ML, DL, and data analytics techniques to examine behavior and motivation and inform human-centered design.

Before joining uCalgary, I worked at the United International University (UIU), Bangladesh as a Lecturer (currently on study leave) in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE). I completed my Bachelor of Science degree from the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST) in the CSE department.
